FAQ’s

1. What are Mutual Funds?
  • A Mutual Fund is a pool of investments collected from a number of resources and then invested into commercial ventures based on their investment strategies.
  • The pool is managed by a team of professionals known as fund managers.
  • Investors who purchase units of a mutual fund are its unit-holders.
  • Mutual funds distribute 90% of their realized income to its unit-holders at the time of dividend pay-out.
2. What are MONEY MARKET FUNDs?
  • A money market fund is a mutual fund that invests its assets only in the most liquid of money instruments.
  • The portfolio seeks stability by investing in very short term, interest- bearing instruments and other debt certificates that can be converted into cash readily, and can be considered as the equivalent of cash.
  • Usually money market funds are considered as the safest for beginner investors while being the easiest, least complicated to follow and understand.
  • Money market funds are considered appropriate for investors seeking stability of principal, total liquidity, and earnings that are as high, or higher, than those available through bank certificates of deposits. And unlike bank term deposits, money market funds have no early withdrawal penalties.
  • The money market mutual fund is recommended as an ideal investment for risk adverse investor due to the advantages they offer including:
    • Safety of principal, through diversification and stability of the short-term portfolio investments.
    • Total and immediate liquidity.
    • Better yields than offered by banks.
    • Low minimum investment.
3. Advantages of Investing into Money Market Funds
  • Competitive returns when compared to banks: The returns on money market funds are competitive as they are obtained by smartly investing in a blend of high yielding government securities and money market instruments in line with provisions of the Law. (Historically calculated average return on investment is between 10 to 12 percent)
  • Profit on daily basis and minimum balance as low as Rs 5,000: As opposed to traditional term deposits and investment certificates, the return on money market funds is given on a daily basis so that you don't lose profit even for a single day. Besides, you don't have to meet a large minimum balance requirement. You can open an account with Rs5,000 and avail all the benefits. There is no upper limit on investment.
  • Easy encashment - no lock up period: In Money Market Funds, you have instant access to your money. This means that you can invest and withdraw from these funds without any term commitments and at any time you want. Your funds are provided to you within the next working day.* Moreover, there are no entry or exit charges on your investment in Money Market Funds.
  • Secure investment avenues with no exposure to stock market Your investment in Money Market Funds is low risk with no direct or indirect exposure to shares/stock market, corporate bonds and long term debt securities. These funds aim to provide safe and consistent returns by investing in government securities and AA or above rated entities. These funds are regulated by the Securities & Exchange Commission of Pakistan.
  • Regular income options available: You can avail options for monthly, quarterly and semiannual income plans if you are seeking regular income streams.
  • Shariah compliant product option available: Shariah compliant Money Market Funds are also available, which invest only in Shariah compliant short term government sukuks, money market instruments and Islamic bank deposits.
  • Tax efficient returns and tax credit (as per Current Tax Laws): As per applicable tax laws, your investment in Money Market Fund is tax exempt if your investment in held for more than one year.
  • Tax Credit: Moreover, under Section 62 of Income Tax Ordinance, 2001, you can avail tax credit uptoRs. 75,000 per annum if you are self employed individual and uptoRs. 60,000 per annum if you are salaried individual.
4. How can you invest in Money Market Funds?
    It’s a simple 3 step process:
  • Fill out an investment form at any of the participating institutions.
  • Attach the NIC Copy, Zakat Declaration Form and a cheque of your desired investment amount.
  • Submit the Form to the branch or representative of the participating institutions.
5. What is Tax Credit?
A tax credit is a kind of tax saving that you can get on your income tax for the year if you invest in mutual fund schemes, investment plans and/or pension schemes provided you decide to hold your investment for a period of one year. This tax savings facility can be availed by both salaried and self-employed individuals in accordance with the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001.
